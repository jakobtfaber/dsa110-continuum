How to Read This Report

Quick Guide:

  • Figure 1 shows antenna locations - identifies spatial patterns (e.g., outriggers vs core)
  • Figures 2-3 compare raw input data vs calibration output - large differences indicate calibration problems
  • Figure 4 diagnoses the root cause - negative correlation means low-SNR antennas are being flagged
  • Figures 5-6 identify which antennas have issues
  • Figure 7 shows the full SPW x antenna breakdown to spot patterns (e.g., all outriggers, specific SPWs)

Color Key (all figures): Green = good (<15% flagged), Orange = moderate (15-50%), Red = poor (>50% flagged)

Interactive figures (1, 4, 6): hover for details, drag to pan, scroll to zoom.

4. Amplitude vs Flagging Correlation (Root Cause Diagnosis)

This is the key diagnostic plot. It shows whether flagging is driven by low signal (SNR-limited) or other factors like RFI or hardware issues.

Interpretation:

  • Strong negative correlation (r < -0.5): Low-amplitude antennas are being flagged due to insufficient SNR. Consider lowering minsnr thresholds.
  • Weak/no correlation (r near 0): Flagging is random or due to RFI/hardware issues, not signal strength.
  • Cluster in upper-left: Low-signal antennas with high flagging - classic SNR-limited pattern (outriggers often here).
{fig_amplitude_vs_flagging}
Figure 4: Amplitude vs flagging. Circles = core, triangles = outriggers. Dashed lines show 25% and 50% flagging thresholds. Hover for antenna details.
